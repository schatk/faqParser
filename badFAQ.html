There are a number of Key dates and deadlines which you should familiarise yourself with in the Academic Calendar. 

  • Teaching start date
  • Tuition payment due date
  • Last enrolment via eStudent
  • Census date (last date to withdraw from your units without payment)
  • Last withdrawal via eStudent (last date to withdraw from units with payment)
  • Centrally scheduled exams date

Teaching start date

This is the date your study period commences.  Refer to your timetable if you are attending classes on campus.

Tuition payment due date

You can find your tuition fee payment due date on your eInvoice.

Last enrolment via eStudent

This is the last date where you can enrol into a unit or make changes to your enrolment via eStudent.  This includes changing your attendance mode (ie from internal to fully online or vice versa). 

Access via eStudent to enrol or amend your enrolment closes at midnight on the date published. 

If you experience difficulties amending your enrolment, contact Student Services no later than the published date.

Census date

The census date is your last date to withdraw from units without academic or financial penalty.

The census date is legislated and cannot be extended or waived under any circumstances. You will be able to withdraw yourself from units via eStudent until midnight of the census date. 

If you experience difficulties, please contact Student Services for assistance no later than the census date.

Last withdrawal via eStudent

This is the last date you can withdraw yourself from an enrolled unit.  You will still be liable for student contributions and tuition fees for the unit, and your withdrawn units will be recorded on your academic transcript as WD (withdrawn).   

Please note: If you withdraw from all of your units in a study period and you do not have an approved Leave of Absence, your course status may be changed to “Absent without Leave (AWOL)”.  You may lose your place in your course and be required to re-apply for admission.

Centrally Scheduled Exams Date

Ensure you book any travel after the last date of the University’s official examination period.

Examinations will NOT be rescheduled for holidays and you will be expected to make yourself available to sit examinations for the entire examination period, regardless of whether or not an examination has been scheduled on a particular day.

Online students will receive an Official Communication from the Examinations Office around census date, requesting that they nominate an Examination Centre and/or Examination Supervisor.

Information for international students 

International students on a student visa are required to maintain a full-time study load. Students who fail to maintain a full-time enrolment will be deemed as having breached the conditions of their student visa and may be reported to the Department of Home Affairs.

In exceptional circumstances (not including financial hardship), students may apply for approval to study less than 100 credits by lodging a “Request to Enrol in Less than 100 Credits in the Current Semester” form.

Students are not permitted to withdraw from units if the number of credits falls below the minimum expected full time load unless prior approval is granted.

Official Communications

As a condition of enrolment, all students must agree to read their Official Communications at least once a week.

Blackboard & textbooks

If you have any units missing in Blackboard by the commencement of your study period, check that your enrolment is correct. If it is correct, contact your Lecturer/Unit Coordinator to query the Blackboard status. Your unit coordinator's contact details, and your booklist, can be found on your unit outline (uploaded during o-week to the My Studies tab of OASIS). Curtin’s official textbook supplier is the Coop Bookshop.

Curtin Student ID Cards

Every current Curtin student requires a Curtin Student ID card. This ID card is required for examination attendance, building access, identification on campus, accessing Library Borrowing Services.

Your OASIS password

You are required to maintain your contact details to activate and reset your OASIS password. 

Check that your contact details are correct

Check that your contact details are correct and up to date in eStudent. Select the ‘My Details’ tab then ‘Contact Details’.

Whenever there are updates to your personal information, including your address, phone number, personal email address, and next of kin ensure you update these details in eStudent.

A reminder that when communicating with staff or areas within the university via email, that you should use your Curtin student email account.

Check your enrolment

Review your course information in the Courses Handbook.

Email a copy of your Enrolment Advice when you have completed your enrolment and any time you make a change.  Check your enrolment advice to confirm that the changes have been made correctly and retain this for your records.
